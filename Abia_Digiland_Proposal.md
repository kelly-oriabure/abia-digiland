## 1. Executive Summary

Abia DigiLand is a proposed end-to-end digital land administration and transparency platform for Abia State. It modernizes the land registry, replaces paper-heavy processes with secure digital workflows, and gives citizens, investors, and government officials a single, authoritative source of truth for land records.

The platform will unify land registration, title verification, GIS mapping, dispute resolution, transfer approvals, and spatial-data exports behind a secure, web-based interface. The prototype already produced by the product team covers all core user journeys, including the public portal, administrative dashboards, registry workflows, GIS interfaces, and dispute management screens. This proposal requests **₦135 million** to turn those prototypes into a production-ready, legally defensible, and scalable state system.

---

## 2. Background & Problem Statement

Land administration in Abia State still depends heavily on manual record-keeping, which creates:

- **Fraud and double allocation** due to fragmented, paper-based records.
- **Lengthy title processing** because applications move between desks without digital tracking.
- **Poor public trust** because citizens cannot easily verify ownership or check active disputes.
- **Weak revenue collection** because land fees, transfers, and penalties are not tied to a real-time digital ledger.
- **GIS conflicts** because survey plans are not consistently reconciled with a central digital map.

Abia DigiLand addresses each of these gaps with a single integrated platform.

## 3. Vision & Objectives

### Vision
To make Abia State the leading digitally governed land administration system in Nigeria, where every parcel has a verified digital identity, every transaction is traceable, and every citizen can verify land records in minutes.

### Objectives
1. **Digitize the land registry** and create tamper-evident records for all parcels in the state.
2. **Automate title registration and transfer workflows** with multi-stage verification.
3. **Provide a public transparency portal** for ownership verification, dispute checks, and zoning queries.
4. **Integrate GIS and spatial analytics** to detect boundary overlaps, enforce setbacks, and resolve conflicts.
5. **Enable secure evidence and dispute management** with audit trails, document vaults, and officer annotations.
6. **Generate certified spatial exports** in standard formats (Shapefile, GeoJSON, AutoCAD, PDF).
7. **Improve internally generated revenue** through faster approvals, transparent fees, and reduced fraud.

## 4. Scope of Work

The project covers four workstreams:

### 4.1 Product Design & Frontend Development
Production-ready, responsive web interfaces for all users:

- **Public Transparency Portal** — verify titles, search parcels, report disputes, view GIS data.
- **Administrator Dashboard** — KPIs, real-time registry activity, GIS preview, revenue view.
- **Land Registry Office Workflow** — submission, review, clarification, correction, approval, and archive.
- **GIS / Spatial Suite** — interactive map, layers, conflict analysis, spatial compliance, and data export.
- **Dispute Management System** — case intake, evidence vault, chain-of-title comparison, mediation log, and resolution.


### 4.2 Backend, Database & Security
- **Central registry database** with role-based access control.
- **Audit trail / immutable ledger** for every create, update, approve, and transfer action.
- **Document management** with encrypted storage, version control, and digital seals.
- **Integration points** for NIN/biometric verification, payment gateways, and the state treasury.
- **APIs** to serve the frontend and future mobile or third-party integrations.

### 4.3 GIS & Mapping
- Integration with satellite/cadastral base maps and survey-grade coordinate systems (UTM Zone 32N / WGS84).
- Parcel digitization, boundary overlay checks, setback compliance, and conflict detection.
- Export tools for Shapefile, GeoJSON, AutoCAD, and certified PDF map reports.

### 4.4 Change Management & Operations
- Business process re-engineering for the Ministry of Lands and Survey.
- Training for registry officers, surveyors, legal staff, and mediators.
- User guides, help center, and a service desk model.
- Launch communication campaign for the public portal.

## 6. Implementation Timeline

The project is planned in four phases over **12 months**.

### Phase 1: Discovery & Foundation — Months 1–2
- Requirements validation with Ministry stakeholders.
- Finalize data model, security architecture, and GIS standards.
- Set up cloud infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and developer environments.

### Phase 2: Core Platform Build — Months 3–7
- Backend, database, authentication, and audit ledger.
- Public portal and administrator dashboard.
- Registry workflow (submission → review → approval → archive).
- Document vault and AI-assisted OCR validation.

### Phase 3: GIS & Dispute Modules — Months 8–10
- Interactive map integration and parcel digitization.
- Spatial compliance and conflict detection.
- Dispute management, evidence vault, mediation log, and resolution workflow.
- Certified export engine.

### Phase 4: Testing, Training & Launch — Months 11–12
- End-to-end testing, security audit, and user acceptance testing.
- Staff training, data migration, and public launch campaign.
- Go-live support and hyper-care period.
